# SubjectSkillz Mentorship Agreement
**Version:** 1.1
**Status: TEMPLATE — for legal review before use.** Adapted from a generic mentorship template into a minor-appropriate structure. A real attorney should review before this is used to govern an actual mentor/mentee relationship involving a minor.

**What changed in v1.1:** Restructured Section 1 to resolve the chicken-and-egg signing problem surfaced by the Track A hypothetical run (Gap #1): the original v1.0 named the Mentor as a required signing party, but in practice a Mentor isn't assigned until after a Mentee is onboarded. v1.1 allows the Parent/Guardian and Program Provider to sign with the Mentor field as "TBD," with the Mentor added by a simple signed Addendum once assigned — without requiring the whole Agreement to be re-executed.

**What this covers, and what it doesn't:** This agreement governs the SubjectSkillz mentorship relationship specifically — the 1-on-1 sessions and group cohorts referenced throughout the onboarding package and Two-Track model. It does NOT cover footage ownership, the creator's storefront, or the 80/20 revenue split — those live in the Parent/Guardian Media & Consent Agreement (Track A) or the Footage Ownership & Commercial Licensing Agreement (Track B). A SubjectSkillz creator may have both this agreement and a consent agreement on file; they govern different parts of the relationship and should not be merged into one document.

## 1. Parties (v1.1 — restructured)

This Mentorship Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between:

**Trail of Joy / SubjectSkillz** ("Program Provider"), the nonprofit/program offering structured mentorship to youth creators and athletes,

and

**[Mentee name]**, a minor ("Mentee"), participating in the Program with the consent and involvement of:

**[Parent/Guardian name]** ("Parent/Guardian"), who signs this Agreement on the Mentee's behalf and remains a party to it for the duration of the Program.

Together, the "Parties" to the base Agreement.

**1.1 — The Mentor is not a required signing party at the time this base Agreement is executed.** The Parent/Guardian and Program Provider may sign this Agreement with the Mentor field marked **"TBD — to be assigned per Mentor Assignment SOP."** This allows onboarding (Stage 3 of the standard intake flow) to proceed without artificially blocking on Mentor availability.

**1.2 — Mentor Assignment Addendum.** Once a specific Mentor is assigned to this Mentee (per the Mentor Assignment & Matching SOP), the Program Provider will execute a short **Mentor Assignment Addendum** — a one-page document naming the specific Mentor, referencing this base Agreement by Mentee name and execution date, and requiring the Mentor's signature acknowledging the Section 3 conduct/safety requirements below. **The Parent/Guardian does not need to re-sign the base Agreement** when a Mentor is assigned or changed — they're notified of the assignment and retain all rights in this Agreement (including the right to request a different Mentor, per Section 3) without any new signature required on their part.

**1.3 — If the Mentor changes** (reassignment, Mentor leaves the program, conduct concern resolved via removal), a new Mentor Assignment Addendum is executed for the replacement Mentor. The base Agreement and the Parent/Guardian's rights under it are continuous and unaffected by a Mentor change.

**Because the Mentee is a minor, the Parent/Guardian is not a passive signatory — they are an active party with the right to be present at or informed of session content, and the right to end the Mentee's participation at any time, for any reason, without needing to show cause.**

## 2. Scope of the Program

(unchanged from v1.0)

The Program Provider, through the assigned Mentor, will provide:
- Scheduled mentorship sessions (1-on-1 or group cohort format, as assigned) focused on creator/media skills, professional development, and the SubjectSkillz curriculum.
- Guidance and feedback tied to the Mentee's goals as a creator or athlete within the Trail of Joy ecosystem.
- Access to program materials, group cohort events, and related resources.

The Mentee agrees to:
- Attend scheduled sessions and communicate respectfully with the Mentor and Program Provider.
- Come prepared with questions, goals, or progress updates where applicable.
- Participate in good faith toward the goals set for the Program.

The specific schedule, format, and goals will be provided in a Program Brief shared with both the Mentee and Parent/Guardian at least 48 hours before the first session — the Parent/Guardian receives this brief directly, not secondhand through the Mentee.

**2.1 — Until a Mentor is assigned (per Section 1.2), the Mentee may participate in group cohort sessions and access program materials, but no 1-on-1 mentorship sessions occur.** This means signing the base Agreement gives a family meaningful access immediately, without a false promise of 1-on-1 mentorship before a Mentor actually exists.

## 3. Mentor Conduct & Safety Requirements (Minor-Specific)

(unchanged from v1.0 — these requirements travel with the Mentor Assignment Addendum and apply to whichever Mentor is named in it)

This section exists because the Mentee is a minor and the Mentor is an adult. It is not boilerplate — it is the part of this Agreement that protects the Mentee specifically.

- **No private, unsupervised, in-person 1-on-1 sessions without a parent/guardian or another approved adult present or readily available**, consistent with standard youth-program safeguarding practice. Virtual 1-on-1 sessions should be conducted on Program-approved platforms with sessions logged, not on a Mentor's personal, unmonitored channel.
- **No communication with the Mentee outside Program-sanctioned channels** (e.g., no personal phone number exchange, no DMs on personal social accounts) unless the Parent/Guardian has explicitly approved that specific channel in writing.
- **The Mentor does not request, encourage, or accept secrecy from the Mentee toward their parent/guardian about anything discussed or done in the Program.** Any instruction to a Mentee to keep something from their parent is a violation of this Agreement, full stop, regardless of how it's framed.
- **The Parent/Guardian may request a session log, sit in on any session, or end participation at any time**, without needing to justify the request.
- **The Parent/Guardian may also request a DIFFERENT Mentor at any time, for any reason, without needing to justify the request (NEW emphasis in v1.1, consistent with the Section 1.2/1.3 reassignment process).** This isn't a new right — it followed naturally from Section 9's termination right in v1.0 — but v1.1 makes it explicit given the new Addendum mechanism makes Mentor swaps procedurally easy.
- **Background and reference verification of the Mentor** is the Program Provider's responsibility before any Mentor is assigned to a minor Mentee. This Agreement assumes that verification has already occurred; the Program Provider should be able to confirm this if asked.
- **Any concern about Mentor conduct should be reported to the Program Provider immediately, not handled privately between the Mentor and the family.** The Program Provider commits to taking such reports seriously and removing a Mentor from the Program pending review if warranted.

## 4. Compensation or Program Fees

(unchanged from v1.0)

**4.1 — No Monetary Compensation (Default).** Unless otherwise stated, Mentee participation is part of the SubjectSkillz program and is unpaid in the traditional employment sense — the value exchanged is mentorship and skill development, not wages.

**4.2 — If a Fee Applies.** If any program fee is charged to the family, or any stipend is paid to the Mentee, those terms are specified in a written addendum, separate from this Agreement, signed by the Parent/Guardian.

**4.3 — Mentor Compensation.** Mentor pay (if the Mentor is compensated by the Program Provider) is governed by a separate agreement between the Mentor and the Program Provider — not disclosed to or negotiated with the Mentee/family as part of this Agreement.

## 5. Program Participation Requirements

(unchanged from v1.0)

- **Attendance and responsiveness:** both Mentor and Mentee/Parent agree to attend scheduled sessions on time, and to notify the other of any need to reschedule at least 24 hours in advance where possible.
- **Conduct:** all parties uphold professional, respectful conduct. The standards in Section 3 apply specifically and additionally to the Mentor given the minor/adult dynamic.
- **Non-participation:** if a Mentee repeatedly fails to attend or engage, the Program Provider may pause or end their participation after notifying the Parent/Guardian — this is a programmatic decision, not a unilateral Mentor decision.

## 6. Intellectual Property

(unchanged from v1.0)

Program materials, guides, templates, and curriculum provided by the Program Provider or Mentor remain the Program Provider's property. The Mentee may use these for their own learning and development. The Mentee retains full ownership of their own personal creative work and projects developed through the Program — this Agreement does not transfer any rights to the Mentee's own creative output, footage, or storefront content. (Those are governed separately by the Parent/Guardian Media & Consent Agreement.)

## 7. Independent Relationship

(unchanged from v1.0)

This Agreement does not create an employment relationship between the Program Provider and the Mentor, or between the Program Provider and the Mentee/family. The Mentor is acting under the Program Provider's safeguarding requirements (Section 3) regardless of their independent-contractor or volunteer status.

## 8. Confidentiality

(unchanged from v1.0)

Each party keeps confidential any personal, non-public information shared during mentorship. This duty survives the end of the Program for two (2) years. **This confidentiality obligation does NOT extend to concerns about Mentor conduct, safety, or anything described in Section 3** — the Parent/Guardian and Mentee are always free to report those without restriction, and this Agreement does not gag that reporting in any way.

## 9. Term & Termination

(unchanged from v1.0)

This Agreement is effective once signed and continues for the duration of the Program. **The Parent/Guardian may terminate the Mentee's participation at any time, for any reason, with no notice period required** — this is a deliberate asymmetry from a standard adult contract, reflecting that the family's ability to walk away immediately is more important here than a tidy notice period. The Program Provider may also terminate a Mentor's assignment immediately for any conduct concern, per Section 3.

## Internal notes (not part of the signed page)
1. This is the first document in the Trail of Joy paperwork set that puts explicit adult/minor conduct safeguards into a signed contract, rather than relying on general professionalism language. That's intentional — the original generic template had none of this, and a mentor-to-minor relationship is exactly the place where vague "be professional" language isn't enough.
2. Per the Two-Track model: this agreement applies to SubjectSkillz creators (Track A) who are receiving actual mentorship. It is a separate document from both the consent agreement (footage/content) and any Track B (Groundfloorsports-filmed) paperwork. A creator could plausibly need all three at different points — keep them as three separate, clearly distinguished documents, not one combined packet.
3. Section 3's safeguarding requirements should be reviewed against actual youth-mentorship/coaching best practices (many states and youth sports organizations have established "two-deep leadership" or similar standards) before finalizing — this draft reflects reasonable defaults, not a researched compliance standard for any specific state.
4. **(v1.1) This agreement applies specifically to Model B (1-on-1 mentorship) as documented in `SUBJECTSKILLZ-EDUCATION-DELIVERY-ARCHITECTURE.md`.** Model A (the course/portal platform) doesn't require this agreement — it's a different delivery model with its own enrollment flow, not governed by this document.
5. **(v1.1) The Addendum mechanism is designed to pair with a real Mentor Assignment & Matching SOP** (see `MENTOR-ASSIGNMENT-MATCHING-SOP.md`) — this Agreement fix only solves the *signing logistics* problem (Gap #2 in the Track A hypothetical run); it does not solve the *operational* problem of how Mentors get matched, vetted, and pooled at scale (Gap #6), which is addressed in that separate SOP.
