## Why this exists

The Mentorship Agreement establishes the contract and the safeguards. It does not establish how Mentor sourcing actually works in this operation, how matching gets decided, or what happens when supply runs short. The real sourcing pipeline is:

- **Primary funnel:** The Subject Media Crew Hub — an active community of 22+ adult contractors already operating under the Videographer Services Agreement, already proving execution standards at real events (PREMIER EVENTS, RED ZONE ELITE, Hero Salt Lake City, etc.), already inside a leaderboard system and shared SOPs. These are pre-vetted adults who understand the operating standard and have an active commercial relationship with the operator.
- **Secondary funnel:** The XpandSports relationship. A $10K investment-level partnership gives Trail of Joy real reach into XpandSports' broader youth-sports network — coaches, athletes' families, organizational contacts — adjacent to but outside the immediate Crew Hub.
- **Tertiary funnel:** Open application from outside both of the above.

This isn't a "where do we find Mentors?" problem. It is a "how do we route already-known adults into a Mentor role appropriately, with the right additional safeguards layered on top of what they already passed to be in the Crew Hub, and with a clear compensation structure ready when the invitation is made?" problem.

## 1. The Mentor Pool

**1.1 — Pool composition.** A Mentor pool entry requires, at minimum:
- Background check completed (per a documented standard — see Section 2)
- At least 2 references checked, ideally from prior youth-facing work
- A short application/interview confirming the Mentor understands and accepts the Section 3 conduct requirements from the Mentorship Agreement
- Relevant domain knowledge (sports background, content creation background, or business/entrepreneurship background)
- Capacity stated upfront: how many Mentees can this person realistically take on (most will be 1-3 at a time)
- Acceptance of the compensation structure described in Section 6.3 below

**1.2 — Pool sourcing.**

**Primary: the Subject Media Crew Hub.** Adult contractors already in the Crew Hub have already cleared a real bar — they signed the Videographer Services Agreement, they're operating under documented standards, they show up on a leaderboard tracking performance, and they have a commercial track record covering real events. **None of that is a substitute for the additional Mentor-specific safeguards in Section 2** (a Crew Hub vetting is for an adult-to-adult commercial relationship; mentoring a minor requires a different and stricter check), but it's a meaningful trust signal at the front end.

In practice: a Crew Hub contractor who's been delivering reliably for 6+ months, with strong leaderboard activity, can be invited to apply to the Mentor pool. The conversation is short ("would you be interested in mentoring a SubjectSkillz creator? Here's how the compensation works...") because the operating-standard piece is already established. The additional vetting steps and the specific compensation/mechanics conversation are layered on top.

**Secondary: the XpandSports network.** Through the active partnership, Trail of Joy can ask XpandSports leadership for referrals from their adjacent youth-sports network — coaches who run programs at events Subject Media covers, families of athletes XpandSports has long-standing relationships with, organizational contacts the partnership opens doors to. These are warm leads with a real referral signal, but they require the full vetting standard since they're entering from outside the Crew Hub's existing operating relationship.

**Tertiary: outside referrals from existing Mentors.** Once the pool has its first cohort, vetted Mentors recommending people they know becomes a real source.

**Quaternary: open application from outside all of the above.** Lowest-trust signal, most diligence required. Should be used sparingly.

**1.3 — Pool capacity tracking.** A simple running count: total Mentor capacity (sum of each Mentor's stated capacity) vs. current Mentee demand (creators awaiting assignment + creators with an active Mentor). **When demand exceeds capacity, new Mentee onboarding into Model B should be paused or queued — not silently overcommitted by assigning more Mentees per Mentor than they signed up for.** This is the single most important operational discipline in this entire SOP: capacity tracking has to be a real number someone checks, not a feeling.

## 2. Background & Reference Verification (referenced by Mentorship Agreement Section 3)

**2.1 — Minimum standard.** Before any Mentor is added to the pool, regardless of which funnel they came in through:
- A criminal background check (the same standard used broadly across youth sports/coaching contexts — a basic state + national check at minimum; many states have a specific standard for anyone working with minors, and the Program Provider should use whichever standard applies in its operating state, with legal input on what's actually required vs. best practice)
- Two reference contacts, at least one from a context involving minors if the Mentor has that history; if not, two professional references plus a documented conversation about why they want to mentor

**This applies to Crew Hub contractors entering the Mentor pool as well.** The Videographer Services Agreement and Crew Hub vetting were sufficient for an adult-to-adult commercial relationship. They are NOT sufficient on their own for mentoring a minor. The additional vetting here is mandatory, not optional, regardless of how trusted a contractor is in the Crew Hub context.

**2.2 — Documentation.** Each Mentor's file includes: background check date and result, reference contact names/dates/notes, application/interview notes, capacity stated, areas of expertise, note of which funnel they entered through, and acknowledgment of the compensation structure they're operating under. This file should exist BEFORE the Mentor is assigned to any Mentee.

**2.3 — Re-verification cadence.** Background checks should be refreshed periodically (annually is a common standard) for any Mentor remaining active in the pool, not done once and assumed permanent.

## 3. Matching Criteria

When a new Mentee needs a Mentor assigned (per Mentorship Agreement Section 1.2), matching considers, in rough priority order:

**3.1 — Domain relevance.** A football-focused creator probably benefits more from a Mentor with sports/content background than a generalist. Crew Hub contractors are particularly well-suited here because their existing work covering sports events maps directly onto what most SubjectSkillz creators are building.

**3.2 — Mentor capacity availability.** Only match against Mentors who have stated open capacity per Section 1.3. Do not assign past stated capacity even under pressure to "just make it work."

**3.3 — Geographic/scheduling compatibility,** if sessions are in-person or time-zone-sensitive for live virtual sessions.

**3.4 — Parent preference, if stated.** Some parents will have a preference (gender of Mentor, age range, specific background) — this should be asked at intake, not assumed.

**3.5 — Avoid assigning a Mentor with any known prior connection to the family that could create a conflict of interest** unless the family explicitly wants that continuity and the Program Provider is comfortable with the overlap. Also relevant for Crew Hub Mentors who've covered events the Mentee's team participated in — incidental coverage is fine, but if there's a closer working relationship, surface it.

## 4. The Assignment Workflow

1. **Mentee's base Mentorship Agreement is signed** (per v1.1, with Mentor as TBD).
2. **Program Provider checks pool capacity** (Section 1.3). If no Mentor has open capacity matching the Mentee's domain, the Mentee enters a documented queue. The family is told honestly: "we're matching you with a Mentor and expect this within [X] business days."
3. **A specific Mentor is proposed** based on Section 3 criteria.
4. **The Mentor Assignment Addendum is executed** (Mentor signs, Program Provider confirms background/reference verification is complete and current per Section 2.1).
5. **Parent/Guardian is notified** of the assignment.
6. **First mentorship session is scheduled,** following Section 3's conduct requirements (no unsupervised 1-on-1 without another adult present/reachable, Program-approved channels only). Session is logged in XpandSports for compensation accounting per Section 6.3.

## 5. Reassignment

**5.1 — Parent-initiated.** Per Mentorship Agreement Section 3, a parent can request a different Mentor at any time without justification. Thank them, don't probe for reasons unless they volunteer them, find a new match, execute a new Addendum.

**5.2 — Mentor-initiated.** A Mentor may need to step back (capacity, personal circumstances). Document the reason internally, find a replacement.

**5.3 — Conduct-triggered.** Any conduct concern results in immediate review and, if warranted, removal from the Mentor pool entirely. **A Mentor removed from the pool for a conduct concern should also be reviewed for status in the Crew Hub** — a serious conduct issue in the Mentor role is information the Crew Hub operating context needs to know about, not silo'd to the Mentor pool only.

## 6. Capacity Planning & Compensation

**6.1 — The waitlist math.** If the waitlist is real and growing, the Mentor pool needs to grow ahead of demand, not in reaction to it. A rough planning rule: maintain pool capacity at roughly 130-150% of current active Mentee count, so there's room to match new Mentees without an immediate queue.

**6.2 — Recruitment trigger.** When pool capacity utilization crosses a threshold (e.g., 80% of total capacity in active use), that's the signal to actively work the funnels — start by inviting more Crew Hub contractors to apply, then leverage the XpandSports network for warm referrals, then existing-Mentor referrals.

**6.3 — Compensation structure (rewritten in v1.2 to reflect the actual structure the owner has built).**

**The infrastructure:**
- **Creators have accounts hosted on XpandSports** at `xpandsports.com/Subjectmedia`. This is where the commercial relationship between Trail of Joy / Subject Medias and the creator's family is administered — separate from but compatible with the creator's own storefront (the cmc-visuals-style frontend) and separate from the SubjectSkillz Mentorship Agreement (which governs the safeguards, not the money).
- **The front door is a Trail of Joy intake form.** Creators (or more precisely, their parents) submit through Trail of Joy first, not directly to XpandSports. Trail of Joy owns the family relationship at intake; XpandSports is the platform that hosts the commercial infrastructure once the family is onboarded.
- **Mentor compensation is a revenue share on mentorship calls,** administered through the XpandSports platform. The specific percentage split (owner has indicated a structure on the order of, e.g., 60/40 — placeholder, not a locked number — between Mentor and Trail of Joy / Subject Medias) is set in the Mentor's individual arrangement with the Program Provider, not in the Mentorship Agreement signed with the family. This keeps the family-facing document focused on safeguards and the commercial structure cleanly separated.

**Why this structure works for a small-business focus:**

The owner has been explicit: Trail of Joy is designed to work with creators *as small businesses*. The 60/40-style revenue share on mentorship calls is a small-business-aligned structure — it rewards Mentors who do good work (more calls → more revenue → more share) without locking either party into a flat-rate arrangement that becomes unfair as volume changes. It also keeps Mentor compensation directly tied to actual mentorship activity, which is what the family is paying for in the first place. Small business done right is genuinely as profitable as scale done badly, and this structure is consistent with that thesis.

**What this means operationally:**

- When a Crew Hub contractor is invited to the Mentor pool, the conversation includes the compensation structure as a known thing, not a "we'll figure it out." That's the difference between an invitation that lands as professional and one that lands as half-baked.
- Mentor compensation accounting runs through XpandSports' platform, not through manual Trail of Joy bookkeeping — that means session-by-session pay is automated rather than improvised.
- Trail of Joy's intake form is the single front door. A parent who finds out about SubjectSkillz through XpandSports, through Subject Medias' Crew Hub, or through any other channel comes through the same form. That keeps the family relationship consistent and the data clean.
- The percentage split itself is the Mentor's individual arrangement, not part of the family-facing Mentorship Agreement (per Section 4.3 of that agreement). Confirms that family-facing documents stay focused on safeguards, not on operator-side economics.

## Exit gate for this SOP being operational
- [ ] At least 3 Mentors in the pool with complete files (Section 2.2) before the first real Mentee assignment happens
- [ ] Capacity tracking number exists somewhere real (even a simple spreadsheet) and gets checked before each new assignment
- [ ] The queue communication to families (Section 4, step 2) is a real practice, not improvised each time
- [ ] (v1.2) The Trail of Joy intake form is live and is the single front door, with XpandSports-side creator account provisioning wired downstream
- [ ] (v1.2) The Mentor compensation structure (revenue share %, payment cadence via XpandSports) is documented in the materials shown to Crew Hub contractors at the invitation conversation, not left to be explained verbally each time
