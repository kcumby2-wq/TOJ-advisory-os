# SubjectSkillz Mentorship — Daily Workflow

## STEP 1 — SHARED CONTEXT CHECK

Before anything else, confirm you're not working from a stale assumption about Track A/B boundaries. If a session involves a specific creator or family, check `Groundfloorsports/trail-of-joy/TWO-TRACK-ATHLETE-MODEL.md` to confirm which track(s) actually apply — don't assume from memory.

## STEP 2 — MENTOR POOL CAPACITY CHECK

Per `docs/mentor-assignment-matching-sop.md` Section 1.3, this is the single most important operational discipline in the program:

- Total Mentor capacity (sum of each Mentor's stated capacity) vs. current Mentee demand (creators awaiting assignment + creators with an active Mentor)
- If utilization crosses ~80%, that's the recruitment trigger — start working the funnels (Crew Hub primary, XpandSports secondary, existing-Mentor referrals tertiary)
- **Never assign past a Mentor's stated capacity, even under pressure to "just make it work."**
- If demand exceeds capacity, new Mentee onboarding into Model B should be paused or queued — communicated honestly to the family ("we expect a match within [X] business days"), not silently overcommitted

## STEP 3 — NEW MENTEE INTAKE

For any new SubjectSkillz creator coming through the Trail of Joy intake form:

1. Confirm this is genuinely a Track A (SubjectSkillz) relationship, not a Track B (Groundfloorsports filming) one — see `TWO-TRACK-ATHLETE-MODEL.md`. Don't default to "sign everything just in case."
2. Base Mentorship Agreement can be signed with Mentor field as "TBD" — this unblocks onboarding without waiting on Mentor availability.
3. Confirm: does this family want Model A (course/portal) only, Model B (1-on-1 mentorship) only, or both? Ask — don't assume.
4. If Model B is wanted, the creator enters the Mentor assignment queue (Step 2 above governs whether a queue or immediate match is appropriate).

## STEP 4 — MENTOR ASSIGNMENT WORKFLOW

When a Mentee needs a Mentor matched (per `docs/mentor-assignment-matching-sop.md` Section 4):

1. Check pool capacity (Step 2). No open capacity matching the Mentee's domain → documented queue, honest timeline communicated to family.
2. Propose a specific Mentor based on domain relevance, capacity, geography/scheduling, parent preference, and conflict-of-interest screening.
3. Execute the Mentor Assignment Addendum — Mentor signs, Program Provider confirms background/reference verification is complete and current.
4. Notify Parent/Guardian of the assignment.
5. Schedule first session following Section 3 conduct requirements — no unsupervised 1-on-1 without another adult present/reachable, Program-approved channels only. Log session in XpandSports for compensation accounting.

## STEP 5 — REASSIGNMENT / CONDUCT FLAGS

- **Parent-initiated:** honor immediately, no justification required. Find a new match.
- **Mentor-initiated:** document the reason internally, find a replacement.
- **Conduct-triggered:** immediate review. If warranted, remove from Mentor pool entirely AND flag for Crew Hub status review (a conduct issue in the Mentor role is information the Crew Hub context needs too) — see `Groundfloorsports/trail-of-joy/CONTRACTOR-MINOR-FOOTAGE-OVERLAP-RULE.md` for how Crew Hub and Track A/B safety questions interact.

## STEP 6 — CLOSE THE LOOP

- Any new Mentor pool entries, capacity changes, or Mentee assignments documented with a date.
- Anything waiting on Kyron (legal review of templates, a real compensation percentage being locked, a queue communication that needs his voice) listed explicitly before ending the session.

**Rule:** the Mentorship Agreement and Education Delivery Architecture are still templates/active-reference docs, not fully operational SOPs. Don't treat anything in this repo as legally final without flagging that status.
